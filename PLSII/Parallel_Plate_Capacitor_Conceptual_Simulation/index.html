Parallel Plate Capacitor Explorer

Explore how plate charge, electric field, potential difference, capacitance, and plate separation are connected for an ideal parallel plate capacitor.

How to use

Choose which quantity to hold fixed, then use the three sliders to explore how plate charge, plate separation, and potential difference are linked. The fixed quantity is highlighted in amber, but you can still adjust it to choose a new fixed value.

Toggle electric field lines and electric potential bands to compare the two representations. The capacitor model used here treats the plates as ideal and ignores edge effects, so the field between the plates is drawn as uniform.

Physics notes

  • This model uses a fixed plate area of 1.0 m² so the trends are easy to see. Edge effects are ignored. The sliders use fixed classroom-friendly ranges: d = 2.5–10 cm, Q = −20 to +20 nC, and ΔV is set by those limits.
  • In hold distance constant mode, changing either charge or potential difference updates the other so the plate spacing stays fixed.
  • If potential difference is held constant, moving the plates closer together increases capacitance, electric field strength, and charge magnitude.
