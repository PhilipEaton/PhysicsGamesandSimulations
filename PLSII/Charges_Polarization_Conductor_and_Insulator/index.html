Polarization in a Conductor and an Insulator

Compare how a charged rod polarizes a neutral conductor and a neutral insulator when only electrons are allowed to move.

How to use

Choose whether the rod is positive or negative, then move the rod closer using the distance slider or the animation controls. The left simulation shows mobile electrons redistributing in a conductor. The right simulation shows bound electrons shifting slightly around fixed positive centers in an insulator.

Keyboard options: use Tab to reach the controls, arrow keys to change sliders and radio buttons, Space to play or pause, . to step closer, and , to step farther.
