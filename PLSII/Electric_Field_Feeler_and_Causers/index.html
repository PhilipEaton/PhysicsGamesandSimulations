Electric Field Feeler and Causers

Place charges, edit their values, and choose a feeler charge to compare the electric field produced by the source charges with the force on the selected charge.

How to use

  • Click or tap inside the field region to add a positive charge. By default, each new charge has the charge of a proton.
  • Click a charge to select it as the feeler:
    • The field arrows change to show the electric field caused by all other charges, the causers.
    • The arrow on the feeling charge indicates the direction of the total electric force acting on the feeling charge.
  • Drag a charge to move it. After dragging, the feeler is deselected so the display returns to the total electric field.
  • Edit charges in the table using scientific notation such as 1.602e-19, -1.602e-19, or 1e-9.
  • You can use the keyboard controls to add, select, remove, and nudge charges without using the canvas.

Display and animation

The arrow lengths are adaptively scaled so the direction pattern remains visible. Numerical readouts preserve the calculated values.

Keyboard movement

When the canvas is focused, arrow keys move the selected charge by 5 px. Hold Shift for 20 px steps.

Distance scale: 1 meter = 500 pixels, so 1 centimeter = 5 pixels. Charges have a 12-pixel contact distance to avoid the infinite point-charge limit. Proton-sized charges at centimeter distances produce very small forces; try nanocoulomb or microcoulomb presets for more familiar classroom-scale values.
