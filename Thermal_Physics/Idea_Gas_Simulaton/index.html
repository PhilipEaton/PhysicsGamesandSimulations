Ideal Gas Simulation: Internal Energy vs Time

Explore how different initial particle arrangements evolve in an isolated ideal gas while tracking total internal energy.

How to use

Choose a starting situation, set the number of particles, and run the simulation. The left canvas shows the microscopic particle motion, and the lower plot shows total internal energy over time. In this idealized elastic-collision model, total internal energy should remain approximately constant even while particle positions and speeds change.

Keyboard shortcuts: press 1–5 to choose a scenario, Space to pause or play, R to reset, S to step one frame while paused, and Escape to stop updates.

Visualization and plot

The upper canvas shows particles moving in a rectangular container. Particle color indicates approximate speed, but the numeric readouts and table below provide the same core information without relying on color.

The lower canvas plots total internal energy versus time using recent simulation history. A stable horizontal line indicates approximately conserved internal energy.
